Lesson 25: Conjunctions

Conjunctions join words, phrases, or sentences. They help ideas connect smoothly.

More Common Conjunctions

These conjunctions help show time, conditions, and contrast in sentences.

Example: Once you finish your homework, you may watch TV.

ConjunctionUse/MeaningExample
Onceafter one specific timeOnce you finish, submit your work.
Afterlater in timeWe left after the show ended.
Beforeearlier than a time/eventWash your hands before you eat.
Untilup to a point in timeWait here until I return.
Ifshows conditionIf it rains, we will stay inside.
Unlessshows negative conditionYou cannot enter unless you have a pass.
Thoughshows contrastThough he was tired, he continued.
Althoughshows contrastAlthough it was raining, they played.
Even thoughstrong contrastEven though she studied, she was nervous.
Exceptexcluding somethingEveryone came except John.
