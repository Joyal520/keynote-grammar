Lesson 2.2: Singular and Plural Nouns (Theory)

Singular nouns refer to one thing, while plural nouns refer to more than one. These are the main rules for forming plural nouns.

1️⃣ Regular Nouns – Add -s

chair → chairs house → houses phone → phones

2️⃣ Ending with s, sh, ch, x, o – Add -es

bus → buses bush → bushes watch → watches fox → foxes tomato → tomatoes

3️⃣ Ending with -y (after consonant) → ies

city → cities baby → babies fly → flies

4️⃣ Ending with -y (after vowel) → add -s

day → days toy → toys key → keys

5️⃣ Ending with f or fe → ves

wolf → wolves knife → knives life → lives

6️⃣ Irregular Forms

man → men woman → women child → children foot → feet tooth → teeth mouse → mice

7️⃣ Same in Singular and Plural

fish → fish sheep → sheep deer → deer salmon → salmon
