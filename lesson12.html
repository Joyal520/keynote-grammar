Simple Present Tense

We use the Simple Present Tense to describe actions that happen regularly or are always true.

Use 01: To Talk About Regular Actions

We use the simple present tense to talk about things we do regularly ‚Äî habits, routines, or repeated actions.

Formulation:

Subject + base / -s form of the verb

Examples:

  • The children do exercise every day.
  • We clean our classroom every day.
  • They play video games after school.
  • He brushes his teeth twice a day.
  • She teaches English at school.

How to Make the -s Form

When a verb ends in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, or -o, add -es. When a verb ends in -y, change -y to -ies.
