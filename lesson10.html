There Is / There Are

We use There is / There are to show that something exists or is in a place.

Audio Explanation

There Is (Present)

Uses:

Used for singular nouns to show something exists or is in a place.

Examples:
  • There is a dog on the sofa.

There Are (Present)

Uses:

Used for plural nouns to show things or people exist or are in a place.

Examples:
  • There are three apples in the basket.

There Was (Past)

Uses:

Used for singular nouns to show something existed in the past.

Examples:
  • There was a car in the garage.

There Were (Past)

Uses:

Used for plural nouns in the past.

Examples:
  • There were many stars in the sky.

There Will Be (Future)

Uses:

Used for future events or situations.

Examples:
  • There will be a concert in the park next Friday.
