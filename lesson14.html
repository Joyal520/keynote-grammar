Simple Past Tense

We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that were completed in the past.

1. Use

We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that were completed in the past.

  • We watched a football match last night.
  • They visited the museum.
  • She baked a cake for her friend’s birthday.
  • He repaired his car yesterday afternoon.

2. Formulation

Subject + past form

The past form is divided into two types: regular verbs and irregular verbs.

  • Regular verbs
  • Irregular verbs

3. Regular Verbs

We form the past form of regular verbs by adding –d, –ed, or –ied, depending on the spelling.

Rule 1: Add –ed (most verbs)

  • walk → walked
  • watch → watched

Rule 2: Add –d (verb ends in –e)

  • dance → danced
  • bake → baked

Rule 3: Change –y to –ied (consonant + y)

  • carry → carried
  • study → studied

Note: If the verb ends with a vowel + y simply add –ed: play → played.
