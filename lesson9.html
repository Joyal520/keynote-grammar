Have / Had / Will Have

Learn how to talk about what people own in the present, past, and future.

Have (present)

We use have (or has) to talk about ownership in the present.

  • I have a dog. It is fluffy.
  • We have a garden.
  • Geckos have tails.
  • He has a guitar.
  • His house has three bedrooms.

Had (past)

We use had to talk about ownership in the past.

  • I had a dog.
  • The gecko had a tail.
  • He had a guitar.

Will Have (future)

We use will have to talk about what people will own in the future.

  • I will have a new dog.
  • We will have a garden.
  • He will have a new guitar.
  • The gecko will have a new tail.
