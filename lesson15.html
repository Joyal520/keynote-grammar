Past Continuous Tense

We use the past continuous for actions that were happening at a specific moment in the past.

1. Use

We use the past continuous tense for actions happening at a specific moment in the past.

  • I was watching TV.
  • She was reading a book.
  • We were watching a movie last night.

2. Formulation

SubjectHelping VerbVerb FormExample
I / He / She / Itwasverb + ingShe was cooking.
You / We / Theywereverb + ingThey were playing.

3. Questions

Was / Were + subject + verb-ing?

SubjectHelping VerbVerb FormExample Question
I / He / She / ItWasverb + ingWas she cooking?
You / We / TheyWereverb + ingWere they playing?

4. Negative

Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing

SubjectHelping VerbVerb FormExample
I / He / She / Itwas notverb + ingShe was not cooking.
You / We / Theywere notverb + ingThey were not playing.
